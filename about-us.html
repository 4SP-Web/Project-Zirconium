THE 4SP STORY

How a small side project for fun at school became a fully customizable platform built by a student — for students.

VERSION 1 — THE BEGINNING

4SP started off extremely simple — a two-page website with a 20-sound soundboard, an autoclicker, and a basic request form. It was only shared with about four people, and the goal wasn’t anything serious — just to make school a little more fun. V1 was more of a personal project than a platform. But it laid the foundation for what came next.

VERSION 2 — WHEN IT TOOK OFF

V2 was where things really began. I introduced a full soundboard page, media tools, playlists (in beta), games, a proxy list, request forms, feedback pages, and account settings. The site had real structure and people started to notice. I only told a few people, but it spread fast — across grades, schools, and even to high schoolers and elementary kids. Proxies were added in this version too, helping grow its popularity even more.

VERSION 3 — THE REFINEMENT

V3 was fully released and focused on polishing everything. It introduced a sleek black-and-white layout with blue highlights, redesigned account pages, and a more organized dashboard. Features like Vern V2 (a more advanced proxy) and more customization were added. While some planned tools — like a music or video player — were cut, the version helped stabilize and expand 4SP’s identity. Proxies continued to be offered here but would soon be archived in the next version.

VERSION 4 — THE PLATFORM

V4 is the full evolution of 4SP. I rebuilt the site around a modular dashboard packed with tools students could actually use. It introduced a Notes app, a Countdowns app, a simple but useful Calculator, and a Shortcut Editor that lets users create custom links to specific pages or external sites. I also redesigned the requests system, combining feedback and sound requests with a voting and issue-tracking system. Tab disguising and a panic key were added to make switching away fast and subtle. Proxies were officially removed in this version due to AUP concerns and the expected rise in popularity. I also successfully verified the site, so when users sign in with Google, it now displays the 4SP name and logo — making it feel official and trusted.
